ECE Emerge Project — Form Your Team by Tuesday May 12, 9 am

EEC1 — Spring 2026

The ECE Emerge capstone project is now open. Your team will design and build a high-resolution digital scale capable of weighing objects up to 500 g with enough resolution to detect a single jelly bean. This is the first open-ended engineering project in this course — there is no fixed procedure, and the quality of your outcome depends directly on how well your team works together.

Read the ECE Emerge Project Document in full before your first lab session. It contains the complete system architecture, technical requirements, and check-off criteria.

Teams

Teams consist of four members. Three-member teams are permitted when enrollment does not divide evenly into groups of four. Teams may be formed across lab sections — you are not restricted to teammates in your own section.

Before choosing teammates, read the Working in an Engineering Team guide. It covers how to select a team with complementary skills and how to coordinate effectively when your team spans multiple lab sections.

Action Required — Deadline Tuesday May 12, 9 am

1. Form your team of four. Confirm verbally that all three of your teammates are committed before submitting the survey.

2. Each team member submits the survey individually, listing the names of the other three members:

https://forms.gle/FgbUYtN1uFU9TQGA7

You must sign in with your UC Davis account. For technical issues with the form, contact the TAs.

Teams not declared by the deadline will be assigned by the instructor.

What Comes Next

Lab 8 — Phase 1 Signal Conditioning Bring-Up (week of May 19)

The Lab 8 instructions are available now. The team pre-lab design document is due Tuesday May 19, noon — before you enter the lab. The design calculations require time; do not wait until the weekend to start.

INA125 Instrumentation Amplifier

Each team receives one INA125 IC after at least one team member has completed Lab 7. It is a precision device — handle it carefully. A replacement for a destroyed device may be available subject to a grading penalty.

Load Cell Checkout

A limited number of load cells are available for 48-hour checkout so teams can work outside the lab. Details at the equipment station.
